Basic principals of Algebra

Reciprocals

  • Cancel out variables or numbers by performing the same action to both sides of the equal side. (Multiply, divide, or raise to an exponent)
  • Find the reciprocal of a number by converting the number to a fraction, then switching the numerator and denominator. A number multiplied by its reciprocal equals 1. This makes reciprocals useful for canceling numbers and exponents.

Steps to Solve the Basic Equation for Future Value (FV)

Basic Equation: \(FV=PV(1+i)^n\)

  • First identify the interest (i) rate. Look for a percent or decimal. Note: If you are using a financial calculator, use the percent, and if you are figuring the calculations on your own, use the decimal format.
  • Identify the principal or present value (PV). To distinguish between Present Value (PV) and Future Value (FV), ask yourself whether the dollar amount is money you have now or in the future.
  • Multiply the interest rate times the principal amount.
  • Finally, time (n) is an expression of time, usually months or years. To calculate the amount you'd have in the future, add the interest payment back to the initial investment (principal) for the number of time periods.
