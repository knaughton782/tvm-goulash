Solve an Equation

Now we know what variables factor in, but we'll need to derive the equation to understand how they relate to each other. Let's see this in action with a simple example.

How much money would you have in 1 year if you invested $100 at a rate of 10%?

The answer is $110.

Steps for solving the sample equation.

Note: If you know how to solve the equation, you can skip this information.
To figure out how much you'll have in 1 year, calculate the interest payment, and then add the interest payment back to the initial investment (principal).

  • Calculate the interest payment.
    • First identify the interest (i) rate. Look for a percent or decimal. Note: If you are using a financial calculator, use the percent, and if you are figuring the calculations on your own, use the decimal format.
    • Identify the principal or present value (PV). To distinguish between Present Value (PV) and Future Value (FV), ask yourself whether the dollar amount is money you have now or in the future. Since $100 is today's investment, it must be Present Value (PV). This question asks what it will be worth, indicating we want to find the Future Value (FV).
    • Multiply the interest rate times the principal amount.
      $$.10\times100=10$$
  • Finally, time (n) is an expression of time, usually months or years. To calculate the amount you'd have in one year, add the interest payment back to the initial investment (principal).
    \(100 + 10 = 110\)
  • You can do this in one equation like this:
    \((.10)100+100=110\)

    Or like this:

    \(100(1+.10)=110\)
