Some problems require a combination of formulas. For instance, you might encounter:

  • Cash flows and interest compounded on different schedules (e.g., monthly (12) payments and semi-annual (2) compounding)
  • An annuity that also pays a lump sum
You must use more than one formula or calculation on a financial calculator to solve combination problems.

What would you pay for a bond that pays $45 every six months and also pays its maturity value of $1000 at the end of the 10 years, if you require an 8% return on your investment?

This is a combination problem because it makes regular payments like an annuity, but there is also a lump sum. Two formulas are required to solve this problem.

1. The PVA equation for the PV of the semi-annual payments:

\(PVA=CF[\frac{1-\frac{1}{(1+\frac{i}{m})^{n\cdot m}}}{\frac{i}{m}}]\)

2. The TVM equation for the PV of the lump sum:

\(FV=PV(1+\frac{i}{m})^{n\times m}\)
