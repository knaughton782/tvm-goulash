Annuities

An annuity is a regularly-scheduled, equal-sized cash flows over a specified, finite period of time.
  • equal-sized: every payment is the same amount
  • regularly-scheduled: payments are received at regular intevals (e.g., monthly, annually, or bi-weekly)
  • specified period: when the term ends, payments stop

Annuities are similar to perpetuities except that annuities cease after a specific number of payments. At some point in your life, you will have one, if not all, of the annuity types listed below. Therefore, TVM with cash flows is very useful in personal and professional decision making. While there are two types of annuities, this tutorial focuses on Regular Annuities.

Examples of annuities:

  • Mortgages
  • Car loans, Credit cards, and other loans
  • Automatic contributions from your paycheck to a retirement account (e.g., 401k)
  • A bond with coupon payments

A Regular Annuity is also known as an Ordinary Annuity. In a regular annuity, cash flow or a lump sum arrives at the end of each period. Examples of Ordinary Annuities include credit card payments and Direct Deposits in Retirement Accounts. Most annuities are ordinary annuities.
