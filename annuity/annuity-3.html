Calculating the FV of Lump Sums

If you plan to deposit $100 at the end of each year for 3 years, how much will you have in 3 years?

Draw a timeline to help you determine the calculations if necessary.

To calculate Future Value, we could just calculate the FV of each lump sum payment using the general format of the TVM equation.

\(FV=PV(1+i)^n\)
Payment 1: FV = $121. Payment 1 arrives at the end of year 1, so it compounds interest for 2 years.
Payment 2: FV = $110. Payment 2 arrives at the end of year 2, so it compounds interest for 1 year.
Payment 3: FV = $100. Payment 3 arrives at the end of year 3, so it does not earn any interest.

FV = 121 + 110 + 100 = $331

Calculating the FV of each individual payment is fine when there are only a few payments, but isn't recommended for long-term annuities. Therefore, we'll calculate the Future Value of the Annuity (FVA) using the following equation:

\(FVA=CF[\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}]\)
FVA = The future value of the annuity
CF = Dollar amount of the individual cash flows
n = number of years
i = stated interest rate
