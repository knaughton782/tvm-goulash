Calculating the FV of Lump Sums

If you plan to deposit $100 at the end of each year for 3 years, how much will you have in 3 years?

Example using the general format of the TVM equation to perform the calculations:

Identify the Basic Variables

How much is an annuity worth at the end of three years if it pays $100 at the end of each year assuming 10% interest?

Break down the question. FVA is the future value of the annuity payments. The question asks "how much is the annuity worth at the end", so this must be the unknown variable. This means FVA = ?. n is the measure of time in years. In other problems, you might see n expressed as a number or as a noun like decade or century. This means n = 3. CF is the value of the regularly scheduled cash flow. This means CF = $100. i is the rate of return. The % is an indicator of interest. This means that i = 10%.

Plug the Variables into the Equation

Once you have identified the variables, plug the numbers you've identified into the equation. Then solve the equation using the Order of Operations.

\(FVA=CF[\frac{(1+i)^n-1}{i}]\)
\(FVA=100[\frac{(1+.10)^3-1}{.10}]\)
