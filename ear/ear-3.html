The TVM equation can be manipulated to find the EAR. Let's compare the annual and semi-annual compounding examples.

  • The annual EAR is the nominal rate of 10% by definition.
  • We'll need to calculate the semi-annual EAR.
Basic Equation with Compounding: \(FV=PV(1+\frac{i}{2})^{n\times2}\)
\(FV=100(1+\frac{.10}{2})^{1\times2}\)
\(FV=100(1+.05)^2\)
\(FV=100(1.05)^2\)
\(FV=100(1.1025)\)
\(FV=\$110.25\)

Future Value (FV) after 1 year is $110.25. Since EAR is the rate of interest paid in 1 year, the EAR must be 10.25%. That rate can be found in the second to last stage in the working of the above equation.
