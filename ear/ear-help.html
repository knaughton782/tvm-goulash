Calculating Effective Annual Rate

Using the EAR equation

Plug your numbers into the EAR equation and solve for EAR:

  • First, calculate the number within the parentheses
  • Then, raise the number in the parentheses by the exponent
  • Finally, subtract the 1 and convert the decimal to a percentage

Using the TVM equation

Use the TVM equation to find EAR with these steps:

  • First, cancel PV on both sides of the equal sign. Insert the known variables, i and m, into the TVM equation
  • Calculate the number in the parentheses
  • Then, raise the number in the parentheses by the exponent
  • Finally, subtract the 1 from both sides of the equal sign and convert the decimal to a percentage


Choosing the Right Fixed Deposit (FD) Based on your Calculations

If you've been able to calculate EAR, but aren't sure how to interpret the data, remember:

  • For an annually compounded Fixed Deposit (FD), the stated rate is the same as EAR. Therefore, the nominal interest rate for the FD from Bank B is already in terms of EAR.
  • EAR converts the quarterly compounded interest into annually compounded terms so you can compare the two FD rates.
  • Choose the FD with the higher EAR, because it will lead to a higher FV when all other conditions are equal.
