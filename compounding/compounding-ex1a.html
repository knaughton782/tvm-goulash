Exercise 1: Solution

How much would you have at the end of a year if you invested $100 at 10% compounded semi-annually?

The answer is C.

Notice that half the stated interest is paid each period, but there are twice as many periods because of semi-annual compounding. Mathematically, interest was divided by 2 and the number of terms was multiplied by 2.

Adding up the principal and two Interest payments results in 100 + 5 + 5.25 = $110.25. That's $0.25 more money than with annual compounding.
