Compound Interest

Compound interest is interest calculated on the initial principal, which also includes all of the accumulated interest from previous time periods. Compounding increases the growth of investments and is very common in the real world.

The basic form of the Time Value of Money (TVM) equation is:

\(FV=PV(1+i)^n\)

Where:

FV = Future Value
PV = Present Value
i = interest rate (%)
n = number of years
