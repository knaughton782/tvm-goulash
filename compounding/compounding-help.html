Identify the Basic Variables (FV, PV, i, n, m)

How much money will I have after a year if I invested $100at 10%with daily compounding.

Read through the equation and identify what information corresponds to each variable.

Plug the Variables into the Equation

Once you have identified the variables, recall the TVM equation that we modified to include compounding. Then plug the numbers you've identified into the equation.

\(FV=PV(1+\frac{i}{m})^{n\times m}\)
\(FV=100(1+\frac{0.10}{365})^{1\times 365}\)

Perform the Algebra

Once you have plugged the variables into the equation, solve using the order of operations.

Remember the acronym PEMDAS.

  • P: Calculate the number within the parentheses.
    \(FV=100(1+\frac{0.10}{365})^{1\times 365}\)⟶\(FV=100(1.00027397)^{1\times 365}\)
  • E: Raise the number in the parentheses by the exponent.
    \(FV=100(1+\frac{0.10}{365})^{1\times 365}\)⟶\(FV=100(1.1051557)\)
  • M&D: Multiply the remaining numbers.
    \(FV=100(1.1051557)\)⟶\(FV=\$110.52\)
    • (A&S: There's nothing left to simplify - division, addition, or subtraction). Round to the nearest cent and your done.
