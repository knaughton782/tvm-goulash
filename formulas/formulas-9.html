Solve the Equation

Steps for solving the sample equation.

How much would you pay today for a promise of $259.37 in 10 years at a discount rate of 10%?

The answer is $100.

Follow the same pattern we used to solve our first problem.

  • First, identify the variables in the problem.
    • This time we’re looking for Present Value (PV), because the question asks how much you would pay today.
      \(PV=?\)
    • We know that the $259.37 is the Future Value (FV), because it will be paid in the future.
      \(FV=259.37\)
    • Remember that interest (i) is a percentage or decimal.
      \(i=10\%\ or\ 0.10\)
    • Time (n) is an expression of time such as years or decades.
      \(n=10\)

  • Once you've identified the variables, plug the numbers into the basic equation:

    \(FV=PV(1+i)^n\)
    \(259.37=(1+0.10)^{10}\)

  • Then solve for the unknown variable. Remember to use the order of operations (PEMDAS).
    • Add the numbers in the parentheses.
      \(FV=100(1.10)^{10}\)
    • Raise the number in the parentheses to the exponent. Leave enough significant digits so that you don’t commit a rounding error.

      \(259.37=PV(2.5937)\)

    • Multiple and Divide: Divide both sides by the same number to isolate PV. Remember to cancel out the fraction.
      \(\frac{259.37}{2.5937}=\frac{PV\cancel{(2.5937)}}{\cancel{2.5937}}\)
    • Adding and Subtracting is not necessary for this problem. The addition in this specific equation was taken care of in the parentheses.
  • Finally, simplify the fraction to solve for PV.
    \(PV=\frac{259.37}{2.5937}=100\)
