Solve the Equation for Time (n)

How many years will it take for you to earn $500 on your $1000 investment if you are guaranteed a 9% rate of return?

The answer is 4.7 years.

Steps for solving the sample equation.
  • First, identify the variables in the problem.
    \(PV = 1000\)
    \(FV = 1500\)
    \(i = 9%\) or \(0.09\)
    \(n = ?\)
  • Next, plug the numbers you've identified into the equation.
    \(FV = PV(1 + i)^n\)  ⟶  \(1500=1000(1 + 0.09)^n\)
  • Now solve for the unknown variable. Remember PEMDAS.
    • Add the numbers in the parentheses.
      \(1500 = 1000(1.09)^n\)
    • Isolate the parentheses by dividing each side by 1000. Then convert the fraction on the left side of the equal sign to a decimal.
      \(\frac{1500}{1000}=\frac{1000(1+0.09)^n}{1000}\)
    • Many people will get stuck here. To manipulate an unknown exponent, take the natural log (ln) of each side. ln is a button on your calculator. Pay attention to what happens to the exponent n.
      \(ln 1.5 = n(ln 1.09)\)
    • To isolate n, divide both sides by (ln 1.09). Then solve for n.
      \(n=\frac{0.40546511}{0.08617770}\)
